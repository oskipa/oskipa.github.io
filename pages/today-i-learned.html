    Modern dictionaries will trace words to Anglo-French

    When I learned English in the 90s, the dictionaries attributed words introduced from French after the Norman conquest as "Middle French." Today I learned that this is an old classification. Merriam-Webster gives as their etymology "Anglo-French".

    Anglo-French, or Anglo-Norman, was a legitimate dialect of French from 1066 until the 1400s.

    This explains the difference in spelling between modern French words and English words borrowed from Anglo-Norman.

    The Aztec symbol for Tenochtitlan had an eagle holding a glyph, not a serpent

    The well known seal of Mexico, the eagle eating a serpent on top of a cactus, is the foundational myth of Tenochtitlan, the capital city of the Aztecs. According to the article below, written in Spanish, what the eagle should be holding is a glyph symbolizing war. The glyph is the combined symbols of water and fire. It looks like a scroll.

    The speculation is that priests transformed this symbol into a serpent, a symbol of the devil, to water down the original meaning.

    RaceTrack

    Race track is a paper and pencil game that simulates the physics of a car race using vectors to represent direction and speed.You draw a racing track. Then each player can move one square ahead. You make an arrow reaching place where you car is. In the next turn you copy the length and direction of the previous arrow. From that point you have the eight vertices around that point where you can place the next arrow. These represent accelerating, slowing down, and moving to the right and left.

    The game seems to be popular in Europe. It was covered by Martin Gardner in the early 70s in his column in Scientific American. It is used to teach about vectors in some countries.

    Musical slurs

    A musical slur is a line that joins two different pitches together. It is meant to indicate that you must slur the sounds together. In a piano that means playing it in legato style.

    Previously I thought it was a strange version of a tie.It is confusing because the same symbol is use as a tie, which is when you have two note of the same pitch joined with the same symbol. When they have the same pitch, it indicates that you hold that note for the sum of the notes. Used when a note crosses a bar.

    Anthony Eden and the Suez Canal

    He was the prime minister of the UK after Churchill. His greatest political moment was the Suez Canal incident in the mid 1950s. It triggered the end of his political career.

    Egypt through its president Nasser seized the Suez Canal, which was previously controlled by UK. The canal represented access to Middle Eastern oil and revenues from its tolls. Eden and his cabinet came up two plans. The first was to assassinate Nasser. The second one was to ask Israel to invade Egypt. Then UK and France would intervene to bring peace to region. They would conveniently then take over military possession of the canal.

    Eden had not realized that UK was not a world power any more. UK owed a lot of money to the US. The US held a lot of pounds. Eden didn't consult with Americans about his plan because he felt he didn't need to. When they did consult them, their eagerness for taking over the canal made imagine US support. There wasn't any.

    The plan started. But soon after the attack happened, the US stopped it. The US famously threatened to call the loans and to dump their holdings of pounds in the market, crashing the British economy. UK had to withdraw, bringing a humiliating political defeat.

    Eden activated the plans to kill Nasser. But by this point Nasser had captured MI6 agents in Egypt.

    Previously I didn't know who Anthony Eden was. I became familiar with him by watching the TV show The Crown.
